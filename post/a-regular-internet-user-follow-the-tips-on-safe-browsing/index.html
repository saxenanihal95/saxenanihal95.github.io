A Regular Internet User? Follow The Tips On Safe Browsing

Written by Puja Majumder on April 18, 2020

Introduction

Are you a regular or avid internet user? Yes? Then, you might know the count of cybercrimes is on the rise. Hence, you must adopt the habit of safe browsing. However, how to ensure the same? You do not need to worry, as the post is all about the same only. So, it is time to start the discussion.

Words Of Caution

Remember, you must remain conscious when it comes to internet browsing. Otherwise, visiting a wrong or malicious website could lead you into big troubles. Your system can get infected with malicious software. Also, it can get access to the confidential information of your device and start exploiting the same.

Moreover, ransomware is also there that demands a whopping ransom to return the locked information. Furthermore, entering confidential information in a phishing website can pose big threats on both financial status as well as security.

Tips For Safe Browsing

Always Use A Secure Web Browser:

Since the web browser plays the medium between you and the internet, so it comes with loads of critical responsibilities. It can largely protect a user from falling into the trap of cybercriminals.

Therefore, you must install a well-reputed and secure web browser and use the same to safeguard your surfing journey.

Popular web browsers have come with great protection against phishing or malware. A prominent web browser can also help you to lessen your exposure to various websites.

Check Your Security Settings:

Being proactive will help you to prevent or lessen the attack of malware on your system. Hence, what should you do now?

Check the settings of your browser and alter some features, as per your preferences. However, changing settings could create some inconveniences in your browsing journey but safety always comes first. 

For instance, it is better to shut off features like password storage or autofill. Thus, you can prevent hackers to get access to sensitive data that could otherwise incur great losses to you.

Hence, you should invest time to fill usernames and passwords of various accounts of your own. But, a feeling of safety will be there in your mind.

Moreover, you should also turn off the “attack surface.” Thus, cybercriminals would fail to access the location, where they can attack your device. However, it could alleviate some of your surfing enjoyment.

Furthermore, turning “cookies” off enhances your privacy online. Unfortunately, lots of websites would not serve you when you disable cookies.

Some other features in this regard include JavaScript, plug-ins, and so on. Now, it is up to you, which features you would like to enable or disable.

Take Help Of Password Manager:

You already know you should disable the autofill option. Then, there must be a feasible option to save your credentials of various accounts, is not it?

Hence, you can use a reputed password manager. It can memorize the usernames and passwords for websites and fill the same when you visit the respective login page.

Moreover, it is also imperative to set unique passwords for every website. Thus, you can stop hackers to access multiple websites with the same password through automation, in case of password theft.

Wrapping Up

You can also hide your IP address with a VPN. Moreover, never miss on to check the site’s security with “https” and the lock sign.
